# 055 Compliance Validation Standards

## Objective
Develop a research initiative to validate compliance standards (GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2 Type II) against workflows and spaces in Nostra.

## Problem Statement
Nostra's architecture involves complex data flows across spaces, agents, and workflows. Ensuring compliance with standards like GDPR (Right to Erasure, Data Portability), HIPAA (PHI Security), and SOC 2 (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity) requires a systemic approach to validation rather than ad-hoc checks.

## Core Solution: Compliance as a Primitive
We propose treating "Compliance" as a first-class primitive in Nostra, similar to Time and Identity. This involves:

1.  **Compliance Policies**: Machine-readable definitions of compliance rules (e.g., "All PHI must be encrypted at rest", "User consent is required for data sharing").
2.  **Validation Workflows**: Automated workflows that audit system state, logs, and configurations against these policies.
3.  **Compliance Artifacts**: "Certificates" generated by validation workflows that prove compliance at a specific point in time or for a specific component.

## Scope
### 1. Regulatory Standards
- **GDPR**: Focus on "Right to Erasure" (Social Recovery of deletion) and "Data Portability" (Schema-based export).
- **HIPAA**: Focus on "Access Control" (Identity-based spaces) and "Audit Controls" (Immutable logs).
- **SOC 2 Type II**: Focus on "Process validation" (Temporal workflows ensuring steps are followed).

### 2. Validation Targets
- **Groupings of Components (Spaces)**: Ensuring a Space has the correct Access Control Lists (ACLs) and encryption settings.
- **Workflows**: Ensuring a Temporal workflow logs all decision points and handles failures securely.

## Labs Recommendation
We recommend creating a new lab: `labs:compliance-validator`.
- **Purpose**: Simulate a "Healthcare Workflow" (e.g., Patient Intake).
- **Mechanism**: Use the **Solver Pattern** seen in `scheduler_lab.rs`.
    - **Input**: A JSON definition of a workflow or space configuration.
    - **Process**: A "Compliance Agent" checks the input against a "Policy".
    - **Output**: A "Compliance Certificate" (Verified) or a Violation Report.

## Key Questions Resolution
- **Workflow vs. Process**: Compliance is a **Process** enforced by **Workflows**.
    - The *Process* is the abstract requirement (e.g., "Must get consent").
    - The *Workflow* is the technical implementation (e.g., "RequestConsentActivity" -> "VerifyConsentActivity").
- **Labs Project**: Use `labs:compliance-validator` (new) or extend `scheduler_lab` patterns. `entities_lab` is also relevant for testing data schema compliance.
